Rafting

Rafting is a recreational outdoor activity that involves using an inflatable raft to navigate a river or other body of water. There are different types of rafting, depending on the level of difficulty, the equipment used, and the duration of the trip.

Why Rafting is Good For You

Rafting can be a fun and rewarding way to enjoy the outdoors and experience the thrill of the water. If you are interested in trying rafting, check out our offerrings below that offer rafting trips for different skill levels and preferences.

Our Offerings

Whitewater Rafting

This involves navigating through rapids and white water, which are sections of the river where the water flows fast and turbulent. Whitewater rafting can be very exciting and challenging, but also potentially dangerous. Whitewater rapids are graded from I to VI, with I being the easiest and VI being the most extreme.

Flatwater Rafting

This type of rafting involves floating on calm water, such as lakes, reservoirs, or slow-moving rivers. Flatwater rafting is more relaxed and leisurely, and suitable for beginners and families. The great thing about this type of rafting is sight seeing, fishing, game viewing.

Sea Rafting

This type of rafting involves navigating through the ocean, including trips to remote islands, snorkeling, and wildlife viewing. Sea rafting can be affected by tides, currents, and waves, and requires special equipment and skills. You also get to see others kayaking and sailing.
